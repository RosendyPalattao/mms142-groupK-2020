Online Advertising

A Report on MMS 142 - Internet Technologies and Web Development
Disruptive Technology

The rapid pace of information technology (IT) has transformed the Internet into the most popular channel of communication in the world today. The Internet’s high speed, user-friendliness, low cost, and wide accessibility have contributed to its commercialization in the form of online marketing – a new platform for generating attention and awareness among consumers through online advertising.

Consequently, such a new medium of advertising has created a buzz on the internet as its use rampantly gained popularity ––– of which has inevitably generated awareness about its existence among internet users. Today, aside from the traditional platforms and obvious tactics, endorsements can now be as seamless as a single, no context social media post by someone who has a few thousand followers. As a result, online advertisements are now more ubiquitous than ever.

History of Online Advertising

The first online ad

At&t was the first company to utilize the use of online advertising

In 1994, they paid $30,000 for their banner ad to be posted for three months on a website, a predecessor of the tech site - Wired. Resulting in a click-through rate (percent of clicks per visit) of 44% compared to today’s average rate of 0.5%, it paved the way for the popularity of online advertising.

1994

New Pricing Model was Introduced

netscape & infoseek introduced Cost Per Mile or Cost Per Thousand impression

CPM allowed businesses to pay based on the total number of impressions the banner ads received instead of paying for it at a fixed price.

1995

Doubleclick

doubleclick launched

This company created an opportunity for its clients to monitor the progress of their online ads by tracking the number of times the ads were seen and clicked. They played a major role in ad optimization that Google eventually acquired them.

1996

pop-ups

One of the most hated onine advertising technique.

Although they are still being used today, due to frustrations brought by it, most internet users enable pop-up blockers to avoid these kinds of advertisements.

1997

Paid Search advertising

GoTo.com pioneered paid search advertising.

This type of advertisement enables businesses to bid on keyword prices for their ad to show at the top of search engine results. This model is called pay-for-placement that eventually evolved to pay per click which is widely used today.

1999

google ad-words

Adwords was created by Google.

Internet users can experience a sponsored search without compromising the quality of search results. It introduced a Quality Score model that uses an ad’s clickthrough rate to determine the placement of an advertisement on the search result page.

2000

Social media advertisement

By mid-2000, social media become more popular

Advertisers saw an opportunity for a larger market and a way to target younger audiences. In 2006, Facebook started with displaying ads and sponsored links then shifted to targeted ads. Targeted ads use the user’s demographics and interests to display relevant ads on their newsfeed. It has become the standard practice of online advertisers, particularly in social media.

Mid-2000

The Rise of native

Native ads are paid ads (articles, videos, etc.)

That match the feel and look of the platform they appear in. They are less obtrusive and usually appear as recommended content. This style of advertisement was first observed in Buzzfeed and Mashable, soon social media platforms started to follow.
Online Advertisements have come a long way. From annoying, irrelevant advertisements to less obtrusive ads with relatable content, advertisers continue to find ways that are more acceptable and appealing to their targeted audience. Because of this innovation, the return of investment from online advertising continues to increase. As a result, more businesses are transitioning and investing most of their marketing budgets to digital marketing.

2010 - present

Types of Online Advertising

1. Paid Search

Search advertising or search engine marketing (SEM)
are online ads that appear in search engine results on platforms like Google AdWords or Bing Ads. Paid search advertising is solely focused on intent-based results. It uses a pay-per-click (PPC) model where every time an ad is clicked, sending a person to the said website, the business pays the search engine for that traffic – basically, a way of buying visits to one’s site.

Paid search advertising is one of the most popular forms of PPC. It allows businesses to bid for ad placement in a search engine’s sponsored links when someone searches on a keyword that is related to their business.

2. Paid Social Media

advertisements that appear on social media platforms
Social media platforms like Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram are paid to promote a business through boosted posts containing offers and promotions.

3. Organic Social Media

brand controls 100% of the channel content and platforms
These posts reach people who have liked one’s page directly and have chosen the engagement.

4. Display Advertising

ad boxes containing banner ads or videos found on the top or sides of a website
They appear in sections that are reserved for paid advertising and are aimed to generate a call to action. Display advertisements rely on their widespread reach considering that millions of websites are connected to Google’s Display Networks. These search engines share one’s ads to websites and apps based on keywords targeting one’s preferences.

Examples of Disruption

  • Goran Dautovic mentions that “Social media advertising budgets have doubled worldwide, from $16 billion in 2014 to $31 billion in 2016.” (2020). Along with this is the report that, in 2018, brands were found to be “devoting 33 percent of ad spend to digital platforms—up from 25 percent in 2014.” (Gigante, 2018) These trends show how rapid the increase of online advertising has become in the span of a few years.
  • The “Total digital ad spending in the US will grow 19% to $129.34 billion this year [2019] —54.2% of estimated total US ad spending.” This domination of online advertising now affects the traditional market. Traditional print (newspapers and magazines) spending is a close second, which will drop nearly 18%. Overall, traditional ad spending’s share in the US will drop to 45.8% in 2019, from 51.4% last year.” (2019).
  • The Financial Times, one of the world's leading news organizations based in the UK, established back in 1888 has now reached a million paying readers. The company is one of the first newspapers to introduce a paywall on their website back in 2002, instead of uploading their award winning and trusted journalism for free on the internet, they adapted to the rapidly changing environment for news media and changed the paper's business model, besides making their journalism subscription based which is the core of their strategy, they also have four other main revenue generators which would be print advertising, print circulation and conferences. They have reduced the number or printing sites but did not eliminate print production, it is through digital advertising that they are able to close on the value of print advertising.
  • Newsweek Magazine, an American weekly news magazine founded in 1933, they stopped publishing its print edition and they transitioned to digital on a subscription platform. Online advertising has greatly affected the traditional media in terms of production, marketing and distribution as online and download becomes the mode of delivery.
  • In 2019, according to the data from the Reuters Institute for the Study of Journalism, advertising is no longer the news publishers’ main source of revenue, they now focus on subscription income, with ads just playing a supporting role, they now know that reader revenue is greater than advertising unlike when they have first started print production.
  • Traditional newspapers are seeing print circulation and advertising revenues decline while digital upstarts and big technology companies like Google are increasingly moving into the business of generating and delivering news.

Disruption on Online Advertising

POSITIVE EFFECT

With the drastic changes that the internet has brought about to the advertising industry came the changes within these systems of which continuously occur as new technologies and platforms are invented.

1. Cost Efficient
Creative solutions on how to be cost-efficient will lead to coming out of this pandemic, stronger than before. This is a unique time for advertisers where we on one side may be moving toward a recession and on the other side have more people online willing to consume online services and products.
2. Direct communication channels.
Traditional advertising was broadcasted to customers but didn't listen to them – clients were only recipients of their messages. These days the rules have changed, we now receive the company's messages and we can ask for more information, or complain about something related to their product or service. After all, great customer service is one of the most powerful advertising methods when utilized properly and efficiently.
3. Data Understands Consumer's Needs
In Search Engine Marketing, a big chunk of consumer data is stored and is used so the company can understand the consumer's buying journey. The data is used to their advantage and with an increased access to consumers, they are able to establish a connection with them before a purchase can be made.
4. Markets are Directly Targeted
Online advertising allows a company to choose the market it serves. If you're a sneakerhead, there are algorithms used to directly show you what you want based on what you searched for.
5. Data Analytics
There are numbers to tell if your campaign is a flop or a hit. There's a saying goes if you can measure it, you can achieve it. Data analytics can give you an overall information about the consumers, your brand, or even the market you're catering.

NEGATIVE EFFECT

On the back of every innovation rides threats and dangers. The digital interface is now awash with advertising that frustrates users hence, fueling a hostile and untrusting attitude towards advertising as a whole.

1. DAMAGE TO THE TYPICAL USER EXPERIENCE
Ads are increasingly interrupting the viewing patterns of its viewers –– desperately gnawing at us not to inspire, but to command our attention. Unlike before, internet users are now forced to endure watching (sometimes numerous) ads at the beginning, in the middle, or at the end of online content such as videos found on youtube and Facebook or before accessing online services such as citation machines.
2. Consumers risk exposure to malware through everyday activity.
Consumers can incur malware attacks by simply visiting a website and without taking any action such as clicking an advertisement. The complexity of online advertising makes it impossible for consumers to avoid advertising malware attacks or identify the source of the malware exposure and determine whether the ad network or host website could have prevented the attack.
